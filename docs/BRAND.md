# Eligeo — Brand Guide

## Name

**Eligeo** — Latin: *"I choose."* The act of selecting the right person.

## Logo Narrative

### What you see

Three candidate profiles — head circle + shoulder arc. The chosen one is bigger, brighter, fully white, and ringed. The two unselected profiles are faded (28% opacity) — they exist to give the selection meaning, but they don't compete.

### Design intent

- **People element** — Recruiters instantly read "people," not "data filter"
- **The chosen one is the hero** — Selected candidate is ringed and prominent; unselected candidates recede
- **No hamburger confusion** — Circles and arcs at any size. At 16px it renders as two faint dots flanking one ringed dot — still unmistakably "selected person," not a menu
- **Category clarity** — The checkmark badge at the bottom-right of the selection ring says *approved hire*, not just highlighted item. A cold prospect reads "hiring tool" instantly
- **Emotion** — This logo feels like a celebration of the hire, not a filter audit. The glow, the ring, the check — it says *we found them*

### Marketing copy (for `/about` page)

> Three candidates. One stands out — ringed, bright, chosen. The checkmark seals it: *we found them.*
>
> That's what Eligeo means — "I choose." And that moment of choosing the right person is what we built this platform to celebrate.

### Technical specs

- **Files:** `public/images/` (logo v2, 3 variants)
- **Colors:** Refer to `docs/DESIGN-SYSTEM.md` for brand palette
- **Minimum size:** 16px (renders as ringed dot with flanking dots)

## Brand Voice

- **Confident, not corporate** — We speak like a sharp colleague, not a press release
- **Celebration of the hire** — Every touchpoint reinforces the moment of finding the right person
- **AI as superpower, not replacement** — AI helps recruiters make better decisions faster; it doesn't make decisions for them
